#western-union 

### Categories of File Types
#### 1. MT - Message Types
These are old/legacy formats.
Here messages are sent in text format + tags (tags ensure strict field structure)
Examples:
- MT101, MT103, MT202, [[4. MT940 and MT950 Message|MT940 and MT950]]
#### 2. MX Messages
The new SWIFT standard which replaces MT
XML-based
Examples:
Payments (pacs and pain series)
- pacs.008, pacs.009, pain.001, pain.002
#### 3. CAMT Messages
Used for bank statements and cash reporting
Examples:
- camt.052, camt.053, camt.054, setr.010, seev.031, fxtr.003, trea.006
#### 4. BAI/BAI2 Messages
U.S.-based format from the Bank Administration Institute
Text file, code-based, used mostly by corporates

### Use cases of different file types
- **MT101 / pain.001** - Request for Transfer/Payment initiation instruction sent by a corporate to its bank
	- A company like Siemens sends MT101 to its bank to instruct multiple supplier payments in different countries
- **pain.002** - Payment Status Report
	- Used to confirm/reject pain.001 request
	- After Siemens sends a pain.001, the bank replies with pain.002 to say which transactions succeeded/failed.
- **MT103 / pacs.008** - Finacial Institution to Finacial Institution's Customer Credit Transfer (single customer payment)
	- Instruction from a bank to transfer funds to a beneficiary on behalf of a customer. A customer in India sends €5,000 from their account at ICICI Bank to a supplier’s account in Germany (Deutsche Bank)
- **MT202 / pacs.009** - Bank-to-bank transfer
	- Payment order/settlement between two financial institutions
- **camt.052** - Bank to Customer Intra-day reporting
	- Treasury team gets multiple intra-day snapshots of cash position.
- **MT940 / camt.053** - Bank to Customer End-of-day statement of a bank account (like a bank passbook)
	- The person in charge of finances at a multinational company receives MT940 from their bank daily to reconcile cash positions
- **MT950 / camt.053**- Similar to MT940 but used between financial institutions
	- Bank A sends Bank B a statement of its account with Bank A.
- **MT900 & MT910 / camt.054** - Bank to Customer Debit/Credit Notification
	- Notification of a debit/credit as soon as it happens (real-time updates)
- **setr.010** – Securities Trade Confirmation
	- Confirmation after securities trade execution.
- **seev.031** – Corporate Action Notification
	- Bank notifies investor about dividends, stock splits, mergers, etc.
- **fxtr.003** – Foreign Exchange Trade Instruction
	- Bank A and Bank B confirm an FX trade (USD/INR swap, for example)
- **trea.006** – Treasury Payment Instruction
	- Instruction for a treasury-related fund transfer (like cash pooling)
